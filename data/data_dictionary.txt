MICROSOFT HR PEOPLE ANALYTICS - DATA DICTIONARY

Table 1: employee_records
- employee_id: unique employee identifier
- age: employee age in years
- gender: employee gender category
- location: primary office/city
- department: functional department
- job_role: role within department
- tenure_months: employee tenure in months
- salary: annual salary in USD
- performance_rating: performance score from 2 to 5
- engagement_score: employee engagement score from 25 to 98
- promotion_last_2yrs: 1 if promoted in the last 2 years, else 0
- remote_status: Remote, Hybrid, or Onsite
- attrition: Yes if employee exited / at risk event simulated, else No

Table 2: employee_surveys
- survey_id: unique survey response identifier
- employee_id: employee identifier linking to employee_records
- survey_date: survey submission date
- satisfaction_score: employee satisfaction score from 1 to 10
- work_life_balance_score: work-life balance score from 1 to 10
- manager_effectiveness_score: manager effectiveness score from 1 to 10

Dataset notes
- employee_records contains one row per employee
- employee_surveys contains 1 to 3 survey responses per employee
- the data is synthetic and designed for portfolio analysis
- attrition is influenced by engagement, tenure, department, promotion history, performance, work arrangement, and salary
- survey scores are loosely correlated with engagement
